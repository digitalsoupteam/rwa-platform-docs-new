# Core Concepts

The Slices platform is built around a few key concepts. Understanding these will help you navigate the rest of the documentation.

## RWA Tokens

RWA (Real World Asset) tokens are digital representations of real-world assets on the blockchain. Each token is an **ERC1155** token — a multi-token standard that allows a single contract to manage multiple token types.

Key properties:

- **Business-owned** — each RWA contract belongs to a specific business entity
- **Pool-linked** — each token ID is linked to a specific crowdfunding pool
- **Supply-limited** — each token ID has a maximum supply set at creation
- **Metadata** — each token has a metadata URI pointing to its description and documentation

RWA tokens are minted when participants contribute to a pool and burned when they exit. The token represents a proportional stake in the pool — when you burn it, you receive HOLD from the pool's reserves via the AMM formula, plus any available bonus.

## Pools

A pool is a crowdfunding smart contract that connects businesses with participants. Each pool has:

- **A funding target** — the amount of HOLD tokens the business aims to raise
- **An RWA token** — the token participants receive in exchange for their contribution
- **An AMM (Automated Market Maker)** — determines the exchange rate between HOLD and RWA tokens using a constant product formula (x × y = k)
- **Tranches** — automated distribution schedules for both outgoing payments to the business and incoming returns from the business

Pools have two key flags:

- **`fixedSell`** — if true, the total RWA supply is fixed and cannot be exceeded
- **`allowEntryBurn`** — if true, participants can exit during the entry period

## Tranches

Tranches are scheduled payment installments that automate the flow of funds:

- **Outgoing tranches** — HOLD tokens distributed from the pool to the business owner after the target is reached. Each tranche has an amount and a timestamp when it becomes claimable.
- **Incoming tranches** — expected returns from the business back to the pool. Each tranche has an amount and an expiration deadline.

If the business returns funds early and `floatingOutTranchesTimestamps` is enabled, outgoing tranche timestamps are adjusted forward proportionally.

## HOLD Token

HOLD is the platform's utility token (an ERC20). It serves multiple roles:

- **Medium of exchange** — used to buy RWA tokens in pools
- **Fee payment** — pool entry/exit fees are paid in HOLD
- **Governance** — staked PlatformToken grants voting power in the DAO

## Governance

The Slices DAO allows HOLD stakers to participate in protocol governance:

- **Proposals** — users with sufficient voting power can create proposals to execute arbitrary contract calls
- **Voting** — stakers vote during a fixed voting period
- **Quorum** — a minimum percentage of total voting power must participate for a proposal to pass
- **Timelock** — passed proposals are queued with a delay before execution, providing a security window

## Secondary Market

RWA tokens can be traded on the secondary market through the pool's built-in AMM. The AMM uses a constant product formula with virtual reserves to provide price stability. Participants can:

- **Buy RWA** — contribute HOLD to the pool and receive RWA tokens at the current AMM price
- **Sell RWA** — burn RWA tokens and receive HOLD from the pool's reserves, plus any available bonus
