# Asset Types

Slices supports three business types that can be tokenized on the platform. Each type has different characteristics, risk profiles, and expectations for how capital is used and returned.

## Business Types

The platform currently supports three business types:

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Growth** | Established businesses seeking expansion capital | A manufacturing company opening a new facility |
| **Startup** | Early-stage ventures with high growth potential | A tech startup developing a new product |
| **Franchise** | Franchise operations expanding to new locations | A restaurant chain opening a new branch |

The `businessType` is set when creating a business via the [Create Business](/docs/api/rwa/businesses/create-business) mutation and can be updated via [Edit Business](/docs/api/rwa/businesses/edit-business). It is an enum (`BusinessType`) with values `growth`, `startup`, `franchise`.

## How Type Affects Evaluation

The AI Risk Evaluator considers the business type as part of its analysis. Each type carries different risk characteristics:

- **Growth** — typically lower risk: established revenue, proven business model, expansion into adjacent markets
- **Startup** — typically higher risk: unproven product-market fit, no historical revenue, but higher potential upside
- **Franchise** — moderate risk: proven brand and operational model, but depends on location-specific factors and franchisor relationship

The risk score (0-100) reflects this: growth businesses tend toward the lower end, startups toward the higher end, franchises in the middle. See [AI Risk Evaluator](/docs/ai-evaluator/overview) for how the score is computed.

## How Type Affects Pool Configuration

While all three types use the same pool mechanism, the tranche structure and parameters typically differ:

- **Growth** — shorter completion periods, lower reward percentages (lower risk premium), more frequent outgoing tranches
- **Startup** — longer completion periods, higher reward percentages (higher risk premium for participants), fewer but larger tranches
- **Franchise** — moderate periods, moderate reward, tranches aligned with expected franchise revenue timeline

These are not enforced by the protocol — any business can configure any valid pool parameters. But the AI evaluation and participant expectations are informed by the business type.

## Token Metadata

Each RWA token has a metadata URI that points to detailed information about the asset:

```
{baseMetadataUri}/{contract_address}/{token_id}
```

The metadata includes the business name, description, risk score, and supporting documents. The `businessType` is part of the business profile and is visible to participants when they review a pool.

## Risk Evaluation

Each business undergoes an AI-powered risk evaluation before tokenization. The evaluation considers:

- **Business description** — the nature and viability of the business
- **Business type** — growth, startup, or franchise (affects risk baseline)
- **Market context** — industry and competitive landscape
- **Financial factors** — revenue model, cost structure
- **Team** — experience and track record
- **Documents** — uploaded business plans, financial projections, legal documents
- **Gallery images** — photos of the business, products, or locations

The result is a **risk score** (0-100) and a detailed breakdown of evaluation factors. See [AI Risk Evaluator](/docs/ai-evaluator/overview) for the full process.
